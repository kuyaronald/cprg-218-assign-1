    Puffin

    Tiger

    The tiger is a majestic big cat with an orange coat, dark stripes, and formidable strength. Solitary and elusive, it roams various habitats, showcasing a striking and iconic presence in the wild.

    Lizard

    Lizards are diverse reptiles with scaly skin, varying in size from tiny geckos to larger monitors. Cold-blooded and skilled climbers, they come in a range of colors and patterns. Playing crucial roles in ecosystems as predators and prey, lizards exhibit unique adaptations, making them intriguing members of the reptile family.

    Frog

    Frogs are small, amphibious creatures with smooth, moist skin. Known for their jumping ability, they undergo a fascinating life cycle, starting as tadpoles and transforming into adults. Frogs are vital to ecosystems as insect controllers, and their distinct croaking calls are synonymous with wetland environments.

    Bear

    Bears are powerful mammals known for their large size, strong builds, and distinctive omnivorous diet. With shaggy fur and a range of colors including brown, black, and white, bears are found in various habitats worldwide. They are characterized by a keen sense of smell and, depending on the species, may hibernate during winter. Bears play essential roles in ecosystems as apex predators, contributing to the balance of their habitats.

    Red Panda

    The red panda is a small, arboreal mammal native to the Eastern Himalayas and southwestern China. With a striking rust-colored coat, white face markings, and a bushy tail, it resembles a raccoon. Despite its name, the red panda is not closely related to giant pandas. Solitary and primarily nocturnal, it feeds on bamboo, fruits, and insects. The red panda is classified as vulnerable due to habitat loss and poaching, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.

    Monkey
